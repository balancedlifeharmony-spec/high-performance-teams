TL;DR

If your team isn’t winning, it’s missing habits—installed in this order: Psychological Safety → Productive Conflict → Clear Commitment → Peer Accountability → Real Results. Build the lower layer and the one above starts working. Skip a layer and everything wobbles.

1) Trust = Psychological Safety

Safety isn’t softness—it’s bandwidth. People speak up, report errors, and share ideas without fear.

  • Run now: 30-sec round: Learned • Mistake • Help needed.
  • Leaders go first: “Here’s what I may be wrong about…”
  • Debrief misses: Fix systems, not people.
Quick diagnostic

5-item pulse (1–5). Track % of meetings where dissent is voiced.

2) Conflict: Heat that cooks, not burns

  • Time-box debate: 10m options → 10m evidence/risks → 5m decision.
  • Steel-man: State the best version of the other side.
  • Label it: “This is task conflict—keep it on the whiteboard.”

3) Commitment: Clarity beats consensus

  • Close every decision with: What • Why • Owner • When • First milestone • How we’ll know.
  • Publish a one-page D-Note in 24h; each member states one implication for their lane.

4) Accountability: Peer standards change behavior

  • Definition of Done for recurring work + peer review before ship.
  • RACI/DRI tags so ownership is never ambiguous.
  • Weekly scorecard: inputs & outcomes by named owner—no “team” rows.

Peer script: “We agreed on DoD v1.2—what’s missing, and what help do you need?”

5) Results: Make outcomes the default conversation

  • Pick 3–5 controllables the team truly owns; review weekly.
  • Updates = Goal → Progress → Blocker → Next action.
  • After shipping: log one system change we made from what we learned.

Team Cognition: SMM + TMS

  • Quarterly expertise map (Owner • Backup • Consulted).
  • Handoff checklist: new owner, risks, first check-in, rollback plan.
  • One-page architecture narratives for major systems.

Leadership Behaviors that Multiply Learning

  • Frame work as learning; model fallibility; invite & reward input.
  • Cadence: Weekly scorecard + D-log • Biweekly retro • Monthly reflexivity (one system change).

Remote/Hybrid: Same pyramid, higher stakes

  • Write decisions down; record short summaries for complex calls.
  • Async for status, live time for debate. Over-communicate availability.

10-Move 90-Day Playbook

  1. Safety pulse + “learned/mistake/help” opener.
  2. Time-boxed debate + steel-man.
  3. Decision Closure Checklist on every decision.
  4. Publish D-Notes within 24h.
  5. RACI/DRI on all work.
  1. Definition of Done + peer reviews.
  2. 3–5 controllable outcomes on a public scorecard.
  3. Progress narrative in all updates.
  4. Expertise map + handoff checklist.
  5. Monthly reflexivity: lock one system fix.

What to Measure (so you don’t fool yourself)

  • Safety pulse (5 items) & dissent rate.
  • Conflict mix per meeting (task ↑ / relationship ≈ 0).
  • Decision clarity % (work tied to logged decisions).
  • DoD & peer-review coverage %, on-time %.
  • Outcome trends on your 3–5 controllables.
  • Missed-handoff rate & cycle time.
